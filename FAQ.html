frequently asked questions

this also serves as a tutorial for auth's RNG :)


basics

how do i roll?
press the roll button above the "total rolls: X" counter, or press auto roll if you're lazy.
how does the rng system actually work?
auth's RNG uses a weighted probability system. each rarity has a base chance (like 1/100), and on each roll, the game picks a rarity from that weighted pool, modified by your luck multiplier, pity counter, dry runs, and other factors.
the game uses a 256-bit xoshiro256ss pseudorandom number generator seeded at startup.

your effective odds = (base chance) x (luck multiplier) x (pity boost) x (dry run boost) x (daily variance x streak bonus)

example: a 1/100 rarity with 2x luck becomes 1/50 odds.
what is pity?
pity is a safety net. if you go too long without rolling a specific rarity, the game automatically boosts your chances for it to prevent absurdly long dry spells.

soft pity: at 65% of the maximum threshold, your odds start scaling up smoothly over the remaining rolls, reaching up to 10x boost at the cap.
hard pity: if you hit hard pity (roughly 1.5x the statistical expectation), you're guaranteed to get that rarity on the next eligible roll.

pity only applies to noticeably rare stuff (roughly 1/100 or rarer). common rarities don't have pity.
what is a dry run boost?
if you roll for a specific rarity many times without getting it, the game applies an automatic luck boost just for that rarity. this bonus increases by 4% per roll after you've failed 5+ times, capping at 55% (or higher for extremely rare rarities).
you want "paradox" (1/2000) but haven't rolled it in 50 tries.
dry run boost kicks in after 5 misses: (50 - 5) x 4% = 1.8x multiplier just for paradox.
the moment you get paradox, the counter resets to 0.
what is hot pulse?
hot pulse is a 4-roll bonus window that activates after you roll a very rare rarity (tier 3+). during this window, all your odds get a flat +18% boost on top of your other multipliers.
you roll "eclipse".
for the next 4 rolls, you're in hot pulse. better luck on everything.
after 4 rolls, the bonus wears off.
what is daily variance?
every day, your luck gets a small random offset (plus or minus 6%) based on the current UTC day. it's purely cosmetic fun -- sometimes tuesday is your lucky day, sometimes it isn't. resets at midnight UTC.
why is there a big counter on the screen?
every 100 rolls, you get x4 luck for a minute. its nothing to worry about, its only good, no bad.
what are anomalies?
anomalies are little rare things where you can "consume" them for a 0.5x permanent luck boost that can build up overtime. this is great for getting rarities that are usually impossible with just raw luck. you get them every time you roll a rarity above 1/10,000.
what is the notification center?
the bell icon in the corner opens your notification center. it logs everything that happens during your session: anomalies earned, potions activated, gauntlet rewards claimed, items sold, and more. unread notifications show a badge on the bell. you can mark individual ones as read, delete them one at a time, or clear everything at once. notifications are saved across sessions so you won't lose them on reload.
what are keybinds?
A and Left Arrow to move pages to the left. D and Right Arrow to move pages to the right. Space or + to roll. W to click (just hover your mouse onto something and press W).

shop system

how do i get points?
double-click any rarity in your inventory to "sell it out" for points. you keep the rarity, but it gets marked as sold. the rarer it is, the more points you get!
formula: rarity number / 3 (rounded up)
example: a 1/90 rarity gives you 30 points
if you have duplicates, you sell all unsold copies at once for more points
what happens when i sell out?
the rarity gets a "sold" marker, meaning you can't sell it again until you get another duplicate. once you roll another copy, the sold marker disappears and you can sell the new copies.
what are the shop upgrades?
luck boost: increases your luck multiplier by 1.1x per level (stacks multiplicatively). max level 100. costs 50 points + (level x 25).

roll speed: decreases roll animation time by 0.2 seconds per level. max level 3 (minimum 0.25s roll time). costs 100 points + (level x 50).

point multiplier: decreases the divisor used to calculate points by 0.2 per level. starts at /3, eventually becomes /1 (meaning you get full rarity value as points). max level 10. costs 150 points + (level x 75).
legendary 1/90 with no upgrades: 90 / 3 = 30 points
with 5 point multiplier upgrades: 90 / 2 = 45 points
at max level: 90 / 1 = 90 points!


rarity magnet: each level you get a 1.1x luck boost to get rarities you haven't got before. max level 5.

point printer: its basically the kinda upgrade you see in most incremental games. every second you get +1 points and depending on how much levels you have on it you can either get 30/sec on level 30, or 300/sec on level 300. there is no max level.

duplicate chance: every level you get a 1% chance that stacks. every roll you're essentially getting a chance to clone that rarity you got into 2. so lets say you rolled common and you get a popup saying that you got a duplicate, you now got 2 commons from that roll. max level 10.

the potions are simple. you dont need help on potions trust me.
what is the auto-sell threshold?
in settings, you can set an auto-sell threshold. any rarity with a denominator below your set value will automatically be sold for points the moment you roll it. for example, setting it to 50 means anything rarer than 1/50 gets sold instantly. set it to 0 to disable. useful for passive point farming when auto-rolling.

mutations

what are mutations?
mutations let you combine two rarities you already own to produce a new one. the result is unpredictable -- it can be rarer than both inputs or more common than both. you unlock mutations by completing the eon gauntlet.
how do i mutate?
go to the mutations tab, pick two different rarities from your inventory using the two dropdowns, and press mutate. after a result appears you'll be asked whether to keep, sell, or pass on it -- same as any other roll. there's a 30 second cooldown between mutations.
what are the odds of a good result?
base odds are approximately 35% better and 65% worse. two things improve your chances:
rarity bonus: the rarer your worse input, the better your odds.
inputs at the very top of the rarity list can push good-chance up to ~60%.

pair bias: each specific combination of two rarities has a fixed hidden bias of up to +/-8%. the same pair always skews the same way, so experimenting and keeping a log helps.
good results are capped at 75% no matter what modifiers apply.
what counts as a "good" or "bad" result?
good: the result is rarer than your rarer input.
bad: the result is more common than your worse input.

a bad result won't produce anything rarer than the top 6 excluded rarities (SUMMER, finished., etc.), and a good result won't produce one of those either -- they are completely off the mutation table.
does the result get added to my inventory?
only if you choose to keep or sell it. after a mutation resolves, a modal appears with three options:

keep -- adds it to your inventory normally.
sell -- converts it to points immediately using the standard formula (rarity number / current point divisor).
pass -- discards the result entirely. nothing is added and no points are earned.
does mutating consume my rarities?
no. your input rarities are never removed. mutation only produces a new rarity on top of what you already have.

gauntlets system

what are gauntlets?
they are a niche feature with auth's RNG! gauntlets are split into tiers, and each require you to get certain rarities. when you acquire all of those rarities you can choose a reward for yourself! sometimes you need multiple of those rarities, sometimes you need this rarity, but it's mainly to reward you of grinding and idling.
what is the global gauntlet?
that's a gauntlet! (obviously) but it is special because it is GLOBAL. every 2 days the rarities needed to get rewards get rotated, so one day it could be really easy-to-get rarities, but in the next 2 days, it could get to very hard-to-get rarities. the rarities needed are usually in the medium - hard tiers to let anyone claim a reward from the global gauntlet easily, but not easily at the same time.

other features

do the rarities do anything?
not right now. they're mostly so you can collect them, kinda like pokemon cards.
do achievements do anything?
no. its mostly there to give a sense of progression at the start. they dont do anything.
what are run cards?
run cards can be generated with the button under "reset data...". when you press the button it generates a summary of your current stats. your rolls, your playtime, your run id, and your rarities. these numbers may not be accurate as the run card logic is held together by duct tape.
what are the daily and weekly features?
its supposed to keep you on the site HAHAHAHA but the streaks don't do anything, it's just to keep you awfully addicted to the game
what does the wishing well do?
the wishing well is a bonus feature in auth's RNG. you can trust your luck and get a chance to double the amount of points you put in. you can only put in the amount of points you have. when you throw points into the wishing well, theres a 40% chance that the amount of points you put in will double, and theres a 60% chance that the amount of points you put in will not be doubled. if the points get doubled, you get the final amount. if the points don't get doubled, you dont get anything and you lose the amount of points you put in.
what is the rarity index?
the rarity index (opened via the "view index" button in your inventory) shows you every rarity in the game sorted from rarest to most common. rarities you've collected show their name, denominator, and how many copies you own. ones you haven't found yet show as "???" so you don't get spoiled. you can search through your collected rarities by name. your collection progress is shown as X/total at the top.

cloud backups

what are cloud backups?
cloud backups let you store your save data on auth's RNG's own server so you don't lose progress if you clear your browser, switch devices, or something goes wrong. it's completely opt-in, you have to enable it in settings.
what data gets stored?
your inventory, roll count, achievements, anomalies, shop upgrades, potions, playtime, gauntlet data, streak info, and a few other game state keys. no account, email, or personal info is ever collected. you're identified only by a randomly generated id stored in your browser.
what is the backup key?
your backup key is the random uuid stored in your browser's localStorage under "cloudBackupUid". it's the only thing linking you to your cloud data. if you clear localStorage without saving this key somewhere, you will lose access to your cloud backup. you can copy it from the settings page.
how often does it back up?
you can set it to every 30 minutes, every hour, or every 3 hours. it also only backs up when the tab is active, so sleeping the tab won't trigger it. you can also hit "backup now" to force one immediately.
can i delete my cloud backup?
yes. there's a "delete backup" button in the cloud backup section of settings. this permanently removes your data from the server. disabling backups does not delete the existing cloud data, only the delete button does.
is my data private?
yes. your save data is never sold, shared, or distributed to anyone. the server is maintained by auth (the developer). backups are provided as-is with no uptime guarantee, so keep local backups too using the save transfer feature just in case.

leaderboard

what is the leaderboard?
the leaderboard ranks players by stats like total rolls, playtime, points, and rarest rarity collected. it's entirely opt-in. you need at least 1,100 rolls before you can join, and you have to pick a permanent username.
can i change my username?
no. the username you pick when joining is permanent and cannot be changed. pick wisely.
how does it know my stats?
the game reads your stats from localStorage and submits them to the leaderboard server automatically every 15 minutes while you're playing. you can also hit "update now" in settings to force a submission. it shares the same random uid as cloud backups so you only have one identity across both systems.
what stats get submitted to the leaderboard?
total rolls, total rarities collected, your rarest rarity (name and denominator), total playtime in seconds, and your current point total. nothing else is sent. no personal info, no settings data, just the core game stats.
can i opt out after joining?
yes. there's an "opt out" button in settings that permanently deletes your leaderboard entry. you can't re-join with the same username after that.

save and settings transfer

how does save transfer work?
the save transfer feature in settings bundles your save data into an encoded string you can copy or download as a file. the encoding includes a checksum so tampered data gets rejected on import. you can use this to move saves between browsers or devices without needing cloud backups.
how does settings transfer work?
same idea as save transfer but only for your userSettings key (theme, music preferences, etc.). it's a separate export so you don't have to pull your whole save just to copy your settings to another browser.

troubleshooting

i took a daily/weekly and it reset my streak to 1. is it bugged?
no, you just missed a day or a week and you lost your streak because of it.
i left the tab on auto roll. why didnt i get anything?
its almost definitely not auth's RNG itself and its definitely your browser. if you have a browser like Microsoft Edge or Opera GX, those browsers really like to sleep tabs when inactive or not opened for a bit. either stay on the tab where you're playing auth's RNG or change a setting in your browser. look for something like "battery saver" or "sleep tabs" or "resource saving" or "CPU/RAM limiter" and turn them/it off.
my file size is under 3 megabytes, why cant i upload it? (custom music)
sometimes localStorage gets a bit too aggressive. try compressing it down to under 2 MB so it can upload safely.
why isnt music playing?
you probably clicked the "mute music" option in settings. uncheck it and music will most definitely work. if music doesnt play still, its your browser. try moving to a different browser like chrome or firefox.
