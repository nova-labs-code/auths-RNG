frequently asked questions

this also serves as a tutorial for auth's RNG :)


basics

how do i roll?
press the roll button above the "total rolls: X" counter, or enable auto roll (the button in the bottom-right corner) if you're lazy.
how does the rng system actually work?
auth's RNG uses a weighted probability system. each rarity has a base chance (like 1/100), and on each roll the game picks a rarity from that weighted pool, modified by your luck multiplier, pity counter, dry run boost, and other factors.
the game uses a 256-bit xoshiro256ss pseudorandom number generator seeded at startup.

effective odds = (base chance) × (luck multiplier) × (pity boost) × (dry run boost) × (daily variance × streak bonus)

example: a 1/100 rarity with 2x luck becomes 1/50 odds.
what is pity?
pity is a safety net. if you go too long without rolling a specific rarity, the game automatically boosts your chances for it to prevent absurdly long dry spells.

soft pity: at 65% of the maximum threshold, your odds start scaling up smoothly, reaching up to 10x boost at the cap.
hard pity: if you hit hard pity (roughly 1.5x the statistical expectation), you're guaranteed to get that rarity on the next eligible roll.

pity only applies to noticeably rare things (roughly 1/100 or rarer). common rarities don't have pity.
what is a dry run boost?
if you roll for a specific rarity many times without getting it, the game applies an automatic luck boost just for that rarity. the bonus increases by 4% per roll after 5+ failures, capping at 55% (or higher for extremely rare rarities).
you want "paradox" (1/2000) but haven't rolled it in 50 tries.
dry run boost kicks in after 5 misses: (50 − 5) × 4% = 1.8x multiplier just for paradox.
the moment you get paradox the counter resets to 0.
what is hot pulse?
hot pulse is a 4-roll bonus window that activates after you roll a very rare rarity (tier 3+). during this window all your odds get a flat +18% boost on top of your other multipliers. after 4 rolls, the bonus wears off.
what is daily variance?
every day your luck gets a small random offset (plus or minus 6%) based on the current UTC day. it resets at midnight UTC. it's purely cosmetic — sometimes tuesday is your lucky day, sometimes it isn't.
why is there a big counter on the screen sometimes?
every 100 rolls you get 4x luck for a minute. the overlay counts down the timer. it's entirely a good thing, nothing to worry about.
what are anomalies?
anomalies are rare drops you can "consume" for a permanent 0.5x luck boost that stacks indefinitely. you earn them every time you roll a rarity rarer than 1/10,000. you can consume one at a time or all at once using the buttons in your inventory section. ah yes let's CONSUME anomalies together!
what is the roll choice modal?
after rolling, some situations (like mutations) show a popup giving you three options for what to do with the result:

keep — adds it to your inventory normally.
sell — converts it to points immediately using the standard formula.
pass — discards the result entirely. nothing is added and no points are earned.
what is the notification center?
the bell icon in the bottom-left opens your notification center. it logs everything during your session: anomalies earned, potions activated, gauntlet rewards claimed, items sold, and more. unread notifications show a badge on the bell. you can mark individual ones as read, delete them, or clear everything at once. notifications persist across sessions.
what are the keybinds?
A / Left Arrow — navigate pages left.
D / Right Arrow — navigate pages right.
Space or + — roll.
W — simulate a click wherever your mouse is hovering.
how do i navigate between pages?
on desktop: use the arrow buttons on the left and right edges of the screen, or click the dots at the bottom, or use the keyboard shortcuts above.
on mobile: swipe left or right anywhere on the page.

some pages (mutations, starmap, runes) are hidden until you unlock them by completing the relevant gauntlet tier.
what is legacy mode?
since a lot of people were very upset about the page system when it was first released, we added a legacy mode for people who still (somehow) prefer the old layout even if it was inefficient as hell. legacy mode disables the multi-page swipe layout and shows everything stacked vertically on one long page, closer to how the game looked in older versions. enable it under settings. in legacy mode the shop and settings are accessed via floating buttons in the top corners instead of pages.

inventory & index

how do i see all the rarities in the game?
press the "view index" button in your inventory section. the rarity index shows every rarity sorted from rarest to most common. ones you've collected show their name, denominator, and how many copies you own. ones you haven't found yet show as "???" to avoid spoilers. you can search by name and see your overall collection progress at the top.
what does "rolls since last rare" mean?
this counter tracks how many rolls you've gone without getting something that meets your "rare threshold" setting (default: 1/1000 or rarer). it's hidden when the count is 0. you can customize the threshold in settings.
how do i sort my inventory?
use the sort dropdown next to your inventory. options are rarest → common, common → rarest, and a → z alphabetically. pretty easy to use.

shop system

how do i get points?
double-click any rarity in your inventory to "sell it out" for points. you keep the rarity but it gets marked as sold. the rarer it is, the more points you get.
formula: rarity denominator ÷ point divisor (rounded up)
default divisor is 3, so a 1/90 rarity = 30 points.
if you have duplicates, you sell all unsold copies at once for more points.
what happens when i sell out?
the rarity gets a "sold" marker. you can't sell it again until you roll another copy. once you do, the sold marker clears and you can sell the new copies.
what are all the shop upgrades?
luck boost: +1.1x multiplicative luck per level. max level 100. cost scales with level.

roll speed: decreases roll animation time by 0.2s per level. max level 3, minimum 0.25s roll time.

point multiplier: reduces the divisor used to calculate sell value by 0.2 per level. starts at /3, eventually reaches /1 meaning full rarity value as points. max level 10.

rarity magnet: gives you a 1.1x luck boost toward rarities you haven't collected yet, per level. max level 5.

point printer: passively generates +1 point per second per level. no maximum level. at level 100 that's 100 pts/sec, at level 300 that's 300 pts/sec, and so on.

duplicate chance: each level adds a 1% chance that any roll you make produces an extra copy of that rarity. max level 10 (10% chance).
point multiplier example:
1/90 rarity at default divisor: 90 ÷ 3 = 30 pts
with 5 upgrades (divisor = 2): 90 ÷ 2 = 45 pts
at max level 10 (divisor = 1): 90 ÷ 1 = 90 pts
what are potions?
potions are temporary luck boosts or special effects you can buy with points and use whenever you want. luck potions range from 2x to 1500x luck and last 20–30 seconds. the duplicate potion gives you double results on your next 10 rolls. you can stack active potions for massive combined luck multipliers — but they run down in real time so use them wisely.
what is the auto-sell threshold?
in settings you can set an auto-sell threshold. any rarity with a denominator below your set value is automatically sold for points the moment you roll it. set it to 0 to disable. useful for passive point farming during auto-roll. example: setting it to 50 means anything rarer than 1/50 sells instantly.

gauntlets

what are gauntlets?
gauntlets are milestone challenges. each gauntlet tier lists a set of rarities you need to collect. once you have all of them, you can choose one of three rewards: points, anomalies, or a temporary luck boost. higher tiers unlock progressively as you reach certain roll counts, and their rewards scale up dramatically.
what is the global gauntlet?
the global gauntlet is a special rotating tier that changes every 2 days for all players simultaneously. the required rarities are randomly selected from a large shared pool, and the timer until next rotation is shown on the gauntlet card. you can claim its reward once per rotation cycle.
do gauntlets have cooldowns?
yes. non-global gauntlet tiers have a 24-hour cooldown between claims. the global gauntlet cooldown resets every 2 days when it rotates. if you've already claimed a tier this cycle, the remaining cooldown time is shown below the reward buttons.
which gauntlets unlock special features?
eon gauntlet — unlocks the mutations page.
abyss gauntlet — unlocks the runes page.
oblivion gauntlet — unlocks the starmap page.

these are permanent unlocks and don't reset between sessions.

mutations

what are mutations?
mutations let you combine two rarities you already own to produce a new one. the result is unpredictable — it can be rarer than both inputs or more common than both. unlock mutations by completing the eon gauntlet.
how do i mutate?
go to the mutations page, pick two different rarities from your inventory using the two dropdowns, and press mutate. a result appears and you'll be asked whether to keep, sell, or pass on it. there's a 15-second cooldown between mutations.
what are the odds of a good result?
base odds are approximately 35% better and 65% worse. two things improve your chances:
rarity bonus: the rarer your worse input, the better your odds. very rare inputs can push the good-chance up toward ~60%.

pair bias: each specific pair of rarities has a hidden fixed bias of up to ±8%. the same combination always skews the same direction, so keeping a mutation log and experimenting helps.

good results are capped at 75% no matter what.
what counts as a "good" or "bad" result?
good: the result is rarer than your rarer input.
bad: the result is more common than your worse input.

a handful of the absolute rarest rarities (SUMMER, finished., pseudopseudohypoparathyroidism, ..., the world, Antimatter) are completely excluded from the mutation table — you can never get or lose them through mutations.
does mutating consume my rarities?
no. your input rarities are never removed. mutation only produces an additional rarity on top of what you already have.
what is trust?
trust is a currency earned (and lost) through mutations. good results earn trust; bad results lose it. the amount gained or lost depends on how dramatic the result was — a massive upgrade earns more trust, a catastrophic downgrade loses more.
good result, 50+ rarity gap improvement: +16 trust
good result, 15–49 gap improvement: +8 trust
good result, small improvement: +4 trust
bad result, 30+ rarity gap worse: −7 trust
bad result, 10–29 gap worse: −5 trust
bad result, small loss: −2 trust
what can i spend trust on?
trust is spent in the trust shop, which appears at the bottom of the mutations page. it has three categories:

upgrades: the auto-mutate upgrade (15 trust) makes the game automatically mutate random inventory pairs every 20 seconds — great for passive farming.

frames — purely cosmetic overlays drawn over your whole screen. options include signal (corner brackets), neon pulse (animated border glow), aurora (color-shifting border), and void (dark vignette pulse).

trails — cursor/touch trail effects: sparkle (glittery), comet (soft blue glow tail), and static (TV static noise trail).

click effects — animations that play on every tap or click: ripple, burst (8-particle explosion), and shatter (flying shards).

starmap

what is the starmap?
the starmap is a prestige-lite system unlocked by completing the oblivion gauntlet. it lets you "crystallize" your current inventory into a permanent constellation that passively generates void shards over time. you can then spend void shards in the void market for permanent upgrades!
what does crystallizing do?
crystallizing freezes a snapshot of your current inventory into a constellation. your inventory is cleared, but your roll count, points, and shop upgrades are all kept. you need at least 100 rolls and at least 1 rarity in your inventory to crystallize.

each crystallization also permanently adds +0.25x to your luck multiplier from the prestige luck bonus.
what are void shards?
void shards (✦) are the starmap's currency. your constellations generate them passively at a rate based on how rare the stars in each constellation are — rarer rarities produce more shards per hour. shards accrue even while you're offline, capped at 24 hours of generation. they're shown on the starmap page and update every 60 seconds while you're playing.
what can i buy in the void market?
+0.25x permanent luck (150 ✦) — adds a permanent 0.25x to your luck multiplier. stacks up to 20 times for a maximum of +5x permanent luck from this source.

Crystallized rarity unlock (500 ✦) — unlocks an exclusive rarity only obtainable through the void market. one-time purchase.

Shattered rarity unlock (2,000 ✦) — same idea, a different exclusive rarity. one-time.

star trail cursor (300 ✦) — your cursor leaves a fading trail of stars. one-time cosmetic.

anomaly cache (1,000 ✦) — instantly gives you 500 anomalies. can be bought repeatedly.

runes

what are runes?
runes are a late-game system unlocked by completing the abyss gauntlet. they drop randomly while you roll — the rarer the rarity you just rolled, the higher the drop chance. there are two types: regular runes (common, mid-rare, rare) and elemental runes (water 💧, fire 🔥, earth 🌍, wizardry 🔮).
rare rarity rolled: 1/30 chance to drop a rune
mid-rare rarity: 1/70 chance
common rarity: 1/140 chance

any rune drop has a 1/20 chance to instead be an elemental rune of a random element.
what is the gift?
when you collect at least one of each elemental rune (all four: water, fire, earth, wizardry), a cutscene plays and you're asked to choose one of two permanent gifts for your run:

⚡ gift of luck — permanently doubles your existing luck multiplier. this is a multiplicative 2x on top of everything else.

💰 gift of wealth — passively generates 200,000 points every second, forever.

you have to type a confirmation string to lock in your choice, because it's permanent. choose carefully.
what are blocks?
blocks are the runes currency. you convert regular runes into blocks via the UTM (universal transaction machine) on the runes page. the default exchange rate is 1 rune = 1.25 blocks. you can also convert blocks back into runes at a lossy rate (you get 0.85 runes per block spent). blocks are spent on permanent block upgrades.
what are the block upgrades?
always triple your luck (9,000 blocks) — permanently multiplies your luck by 3x.

more blocks! (15,000 blocks) — changes the rune-to-block exchange rate from 1.25 to 10 per rune.

anomaly anomaly anomaly (17,000 blocks) — passively generates +50 anomalies every 2 seconds.

dopamine attack (20,000 blocks) — automatically clicks the roll button 3 times per second. yes, 3 times per second.

double clover (50,000 blocks) — instantly grants 50,000,000 anomalies and permanently adds 4x luck.

all block upgrades are permanent and don't reset.

wishing well

what does the wishing well do?
the wishing well lets you gamble your points for a chance to double them. throw in any amount of points you have. there's a 40% chance you get back double what you threw in, and a 60% chance you lose it all. the well has a cooldown between uses. your stats (total thrown, total received, times thrown, success rate) are tracked.
is there any strategy to the wishing well?
not really — it's pure RNG at 40/60 odds. the house wins on average, so it's a fun way to blow points rather than a reliable path to wealth. the quick-bet buttons (10, 50, 100, 500) are there if you just want to toss in a small amount.

settings & theme editor

what settings are available?
mute music — turns off background music.
legacy layout — switches to a single-page vertical layout instead of multi-page swipe.
raw numbers — shows exact numbers instead of abbreviated (e.g. 1,000,000 instead of 1M).
background music — choose from several built-in tracks or upload your own (up to 100MB total).
roll sound — adds an optional sound effect on each roll (none, click, whoosh, or coin).
"rare" threshold — defines what counts as rare for confetti, highlights, and the rolls-since-rare counter.
auto-sell threshold — automatically sells rarities below a denominator value.
what is the theme editor?
the theme editor (opened via the "open theme editor" button in settings) lets you customize nearly every visual aspect of the game. changes only affect the main game page — not the leaderboard, FAQ, or credits pages.
colors: background, text, panels, overlays, borders, buttons, accent, points, achievement highlight
shape: border radius (square to pill), border width, text size
font: default monospace, serif, monospace, or opendyslexic
inventory style: compact, comfy, or card grid
spinner style: slot machine, fade, or none
roll button size: normal, small, large, or huge
custom roll button text
background: solid color, linear/radial gradient, or uploaded image
background pattern: none, dots, grid, waves, diagonal lines
seasonal particles: none, winter, spring, summer, fall (with low/medium/high density)
effects: frosted glass/blur panels, compact mode, hide cursor, hide luck breakdown, reduce motion, high contrast, larger click targets, RGB background, wacky text size, ADHD mode
thresholds: confetti threshold, cutscene threshold
what are theme presets?
the theme editor comes with several built-in presets: default, material you, light, glassmorphism, terminal, and solarized. you can also save your own custom presets, import/export themes as JSON, and auto-adapt the rest of the color palette from just a background color using the "auto-adapt other colors to background" button. some community presets may be broken — use them at your own risk.

save and settings transfer

how does save transfer work?
the save transfer section in settings bundles your save data into an encoded string. it includes a checksum so tampered data gets rejected on import. use it to move saves between browsers or devices without needing cloud backups. you can copy the code to clipboard or download it as a .txt file.
how does settings transfer work?
same idea as save transfer but only for your settings (theme, music preferences, etc.). it's a separate export so you can copy just your visual setup to another browser without touching your save data.

cloud backups

what are cloud backups?
cloud backups store your save data on auth's RNG's own server so you don't lose progress if you clear your browser, switch devices, or something goes wrong. it's completely opt-in — you have to enable it in settings.
what data gets stored?
your inventory, roll count, achievements, anomalies, shop upgrades, potions, playtime, gauntlet data, streak info, and a few other game state keys. no account, email, or personal info is ever collected. you're identified only by a randomly generated ID stored in your browser. IM NOT SKETCHY
what is the backup key?
your backup key is the random UUID stored in localStorage under "cloudBackupUid". it's the only thing linking you to your cloud data. if you clear localStorage without saving this key, you'll lose access to your cloud backup. you can copy it from the settings page.
how often does it back up?
you can set backups to every 30 minutes, every hour, or every 3 hours. it only backs up when the tab is active. you can also hit "backup now" to force an immediate backup.
can i delete my cloud backup?
yes. there's a "delete backup" button in the cloud backup section. this permanently removes your data from the server. disabling backups does not delete existing cloud data — only the delete button does.

leaderboard

what is the leaderboard?
the leaderboard ranks players by stats like total rolls, playtime, points, and rarest rarity collected. it's entirely opt-in. you need at least 1,100 rolls to join and must pick a permanent username.
can i change my username?
no. the username you pick when joining is permanent and cannot be changed. choose wisely.
how does it know my stats?
the game reads your stats from localStorage and submits them to the leaderboard server automatically every 15 minutes while you're playing. you can also hit "update now" in settings to force a submission. it uses the same random UID as cloud backups, so you only have one identity across both systems.
what stats get submitted to the leaderboard?
total rolls, total rarities collected, your rarest rarity (name and denominator), total playtime in seconds, and your current point total. nothing else is sent — no personal info, no settings data.
can i opt out after joining?
yes. there's an "opt out" button in settings that permanently deletes your leaderboard entry. you can't re-join with the same username after opting out.

other features

do rarities do anything?
not right now. they're mostly there to collect, kind of like pokemon cards.
do achievements do anything?
no. they're there to give a sense of progression at the start. they don't provide any gameplay benefit.
what are run cards?
run cards can be generated via the button under "reset data..." on the last page. pressing it generates a shareable summary of your current stats: rolls, playtime, run ID, and your rarities. the run card logic is a bit janky, so numbers may not always be perfectly accurate.
what are the daily and weekly features?
you can claim a daily reward once per day and a weekly reward once per week. claiming them builds a streak. the streaks don't currently do anything special — it's mostly just there to keep you coming back.
what is the nightly build?
the nightly build (nightly.authsrng.xyz) is an unstable preview of upcoming features. it may have bugs, broken features, or incomplete content. do not trust your save data to the nightly build — it could be corrupted or wiped without warning. it's intended for testers and the developer only.

troubleshooting

i took a daily/weekly and it reset my streak to 1. is it bugged?
no, you just missed a day or a week and lost your streak.
i left the tab on auto roll. why didn't i get anything?
almost definitely your browser throttling or sleeping the tab. browsers like Microsoft Edge and Opera GX aggressively sleep inactive tabs. look for settings like "battery saver," "sleep tabs," "resource saving," or "CPU/RAM limiter" and turn them off. or just stay on the tab while it rolls.
why can't i upload any more songs?
you hit the 100MB custom music limit. congratulations on being that dedicated, go celebrate by taking a shower. you'll need to remove some existing custom tracks to free up space before you can upload more. :)
why isn't music playing?
you probably have "mute music" checked in settings. uncheck it and music should start. if it still doesn't play, it's likely a browser restriction — try a different browser like Chrome or Firefox.
my save transfer isn't working. what do i do?
if the built-in save transfer fails, there's a manual console method. open the browser console on the old site (press F12, click Console, or right-click → Inspect → Console).

before pasting anything, type this and press enter:
allow pasting
some browsers block console pasting by default. this unlocks it. you may not see any response — that's normal.

why is it that when i visit the site with a custom song saved from last session, no song plays?
your browser being goofy. go to settings, check "mute music," save, then uncheck it. that usually fixes it!
i found a bug. where do i report it?
join the nerimity server and post in bugs, join the discord and post in #bugs, email hello@authsrng.xyz, or open a github issue at github.com/auth1ery/auths-RNG.
